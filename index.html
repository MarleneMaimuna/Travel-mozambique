Overview

Mozambique, officially the Republic of Mozambique, is a country located in southeastern Africa, bordered by the Indian Ocean to the east, Tanzania to the north, Malawi and Zambia to the northwest, Zimbabwe to the west, and Eswatini and South Africa to the southwest.

With more than 2,400 km of coastline, Mozambique blends postcard-worthy islands, coral reefs, and mangrove lagoons with vibrant cities like Maputo and historical gems such as Ilha de Moçambique.

Tourist Highlights — Bazaruto Archipelago

Crystal-clear waters with a unique turquoise tone make the Bazaruto Archipelago a true refuge. Formed by five main islands, travelers encounter multicolored fish, wild dolphins, and sea turtles, as well as a variety of bird species and pink flamingos.

Gorongosa National Park

Set within the Great Rift Valley and known as the heart of Mozambique, Gorongosa bursts with life. Its landscapes span savannas, tropical and montane forests, Lake Urema, and crystal-clear waterfalls.

Quirimbas Archipelago

A set of 32 idyllic islands forms the Quirimbas Archipelago. White-sand beaches and turquoise seas attract honeymooners and nature lovers alike, with a nearly untouched environment.

Travel Tips & Practical Info

  • Best time: Dry season (May–October) for clear seas and easier park access.
  • Top bases: Maputo (city & food), Vilankulo (Bazaruto), Pemba/Ibo (Quirimbas), Chimoio (Gorongosa).
  • Food to try: Peri-peri prawns, matapa (cassava leaves with coconut), grilled fish.
  • Language: Portuguese is official; English common in tourist hubs.
